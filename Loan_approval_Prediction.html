Accuracy Comparison Table¶

In [60]:
accuracy_table = pd.DataFrame({
    'Before SMOTE': before_smote,
    'After SMOTE': after_smote
})

accuracy_table.reset_index(inplace=True)
accuracy_table.rename(columns={'index':'Model'}, inplace=True)

accuracy_table['Change'] = (
    accuracy_table['After SMOTE'] -
    accuracy_table['Before SMOTE'])

accuracy_table = accuracy_table.round(2)

print(accuracy_table.to_string(index=False))

                 Model  Before SMOTE  After SMOTE  Change
RandomForestClassifier         82.50        73.33   -9.17
  KNeighborsClassifier         63.75        55.42   -8.33
                   SVC         69.17        61.67   -7.50
    LogisticRegression         80.83        71.67   -9.17
Accuracy decreased after SMOTE, which is normal as prior to SMOTE data was: -Dataset was imbalanced. -Majority class (1) had many more samples than minority class (0). -Models became biased toward predicting the majority class. -Accuracy looked artificially high. After SMOTE dataset: - The model becomes less biased. - Minority class prediction improves. - Recall for class 0 generally increases. - Model becomes fairer and more realistic. In imbalanced datasets, Accuracy alone is not enough. Precision, Recall, F1-score are more important. Modelwise Analysis 1. Random Forest Classifier - 82.50(before) 73.33 (after) with a change of -9.17 It is still the best performing model as it handles imbalance better than other models.Accuracy decreased because it now tries to classify minority class correctly. 2. Logistic Regression - 80.83(before) 71.67(after) with a change of -9.17 It gives a Good stable performance.Works well after balancing and is more interpretable. 3. KNeighborsClassifier - 63.75(before) 55.42(after) with a change of -8.33 KNN is sensitive to synthetic samples created by SMOTE.Synthetic neighbors may confuse the model.Therefore,KNeighborsClassifier is Not ideal here 4. SVC - 69.17(before) 61.67(after) with a change of -7.50 SVC performance dropped heavily. SMOTE may have introduced overlapping boundary points.SVC struggles when class boundaries become noisy. Weakest model after SMOTE. Conclusion: Before applying SMOTE, the models achieved higher accuracy due to class imbalance in the dataset. After applying SMOTE, the dataset became balanced, resulting in slightly lower accuracy but improved minority class prediction and overall model fairness. Among all models, Random Forest Classifier performed best both before and after SMOTE. Therefore, Misclassifying rejected loans as approved can be risky and Minority class prediction matters.
